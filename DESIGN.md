# Alpine Dusk — a visual philosophy

*The governing aesthetic of the Trip Budget app. Every surface, motion, and mark answers to it.*

---

## The movement

**Alpine Dusk** is the light of a mountain valley in the last hour before night — when snow still holds the sun's warmth on one face and the sky has already turned to cold indigo on the other. It is a philosophy of *two temperatures held in one frame*: warmth and cool, intimacy and altitude, the human and the immense. The app lives in that hour. Its body is warm limestone paper, the colour of a chalet wall at golden hour; its identity moments — the hero, the countdown, the navigation — drop into deep night-navy, the sky above the peaks. Nothing is pure white, nothing is pure black. Everything is dusk.

## Space and form

Form is **layered atmosphere, not flat shape**. A mountain is never one silhouette — it is three: a far range dissolved into haze, a mid ridge holding its edge, a near slope dark and certain. Depth is built the way a master watercolourist builds it, distant things lighter and cooler, near things saturated and grounded, so the eye reads *air* between the layers. Surfaces float as panes of frosted glass over this atmosphere — translucent, edge-lit, catching the light behind them. Space is generous; the composition breathes so a single number or a single peak can carry weight.

## Colour and material

The palette is **disciplined dusk**: limestone paper, night indigo, and a snow-warm peach where the last light lands, with a single cool blue as the working accent and green/red reserved only for meaning — under budget, over budget. Colour is never decoration; it is either atmosphere or information. Material is glass and paper: the frost of `backdrop-blur`, the tooth of a limestone surface, the soft inner glow along a translucent edge that sells the pane. Every gradient is a sky; every shadow is two shadows — a tight contact line and a soft ambient fall — the way real light behaves.

## Scale, rhythm, motion

Rhythm is **calm punctuated by one bold gesture per view** — the giant countdown, the single hero number — everything else quiet and subordinate. Motion is physics, never decoration: numbers count up as if settling, bars fill with a spring's ease, surfaces rise a few pixels as they enter. Nothing loops forever, nothing demands attention it hasn't earned. Interaction answers instantly — a press yields, a glass lifts toward the finger — with the unhurried confidence of an instrument built by someone who has made a thousand of them.

## The standard

This is work meant to look **meticulously crafted** — as though every alignment was refined a dozen times, every colour calibrated against sunlight and shade, every curve of every peak drawn with the patience of a cartographer. It should read as the product of deep expertise and painstaking attention, master-level execution in service of a couple tracking money on a mountain. The user should never see the labour — only feel that they are holding something made with care by someone at the top of their field.

*Text is a rare, deliberate accent. The design carries the meaning.*
